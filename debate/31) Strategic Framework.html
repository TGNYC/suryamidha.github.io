31) Strategic Framework

Topic

How to write a strategic framework

Lecture

  • Sillogistic Frameworks
    • One primary argument and many sibsidary arguments
    • Allow controlled offense on framework debate
    • Written from the perspective of the 2AR
    • Implicit weighing in the 1AR
    • Judges are confused from the framework debate
    • Far less about the debate
  • Stack Frameworks
    • Many arguments that support a moral theory with no prioritization
    • Not sure with arguments have the most viability
    • So much potential for NEG offense
    • No internal prioritization
    • Easy for them to reprioratize debate
    • Logical priority
    • Not picking what's hardest to turn
    • Internal or implicity heirarchy
    • Force you to go for arguments you don't want to debate
  • Framework debate
    • Explaining FW to judge
    • Ex. No real decisive arguments against UTIL
    • Who won the framework debate
    • Comparative
    • Reasons why our framework is comparatively better than our opponents
    • Make a list of common NEG framework
      • Most Common Neg
      • Best Neg
      • What neg is going to run
    • Customize framework to your opponent's framework
    • Find contention sthat are true and construct framework around it
    • Unturnable issues
    • Force them to debate you on framework
    • Start with cont research
    • Make a FW around it
    • Write conts before fw
    • Primary Syllogism
      • Do a bunch of research on FW
      • Very hard to turn
        • Don't use Cummiskey
        • Very hard for them to link in
      • Appeal to something that's not debate on
        • Morality = x
          • Very hard to contest
          • Internal contradictions if responded to
          • Link story as short as possible
          • Avoid being impact justified
        • My vc alone provides x
          • It's morally good to figure out moral truths
          • Your vc is inherently good, not it gets good things
    • Standard analysis
      • Clarify anything unclear in standard
        • What VC isn't
      • What links back to VC
        • Clarify what offense links back to VC
        • Warrant for why primary syllogism is upheld
      • Intuitiveness in Value Criterion
        • Why it evaluates the way it does
      • Additional reasons to prefer
        • Least turnable arguments
        • Want reasons that prefer to preclude and interact with common Neg arguments
        • Denied X is the most important thing
      • Reasons to prefer for best VC, Opponent Specific VC, common VC
      • Prerequisite
        • My fw is a prerequisites
    • Pre empts
      • Arguments people are making
      • Never look the same the beginning or the end
      • As aspects of additional arguments to prefer
    • Rewrite your contentions
