Topic

How do you give a good 1AR?

Lecture

  • Overview
    • 1AR doesn't win a debate
    • 4 mins responding to a 7 min speech
    • Keep yourself alive
    • Create a lot of offense
    • Collapse doors and think big picture in 2AR
  • Order of the 1AR
    • Theory
      • If you drop theory, you loose
      • Spend the necessary time on
      • Can't weigh other things with theory
      • Spend as little time on it as possible
      • Get rid of it very quickly
      • Anything longer than 1:30 is too long
      • Aim for 1:00
      • 45 secs if crappy argument
      • Make a framework argument
        • Reasonability, reject theory, RVIs
        • RVIs (not abusive) for one theory shell
        • Reasoability (good is good enough) / Reject Theory for a ton of theory shells
      • RVIS are really strategic on the 1AR
    • Case (1AC)
      • Most important part
      • Explaining how the parts you read from your case interact with NEG case
      • Two parts
        • The Overview (30 to 40 seconds, maybe 1 min if really complicated)
          • Give an overview that explains their AFF
          • All weighing and turning
          • Arguments
          • Telling the story of their AFF
          • Functionally extend all your offense
          • No disjointed, confused extention
          • Interact your arguments with their arguments
          • How your arguments turns Neg's arguments
          • Why is your AFF important (what does it solve)?
          • Why did you write your AFF?
        • The Line-By-Line (Dependent on NC)
          • Answer every relevant NC argument in order
          • Signpost very well
          • Singpost, argument, signpost, argument
          • Answe every argument very fast
    • Ks or Counterplans
      • Ks on top of counterplans
      • Answer advocacies
      • If you drop a K, you loose
      • If you drop a counterplan, they solve for AFF
      • Always perm
      • Read solvencey deficit
      • Get through them pretty quickly
    • NC
      • NCs have seperate frameworks
      • Can't weigh under the NC's framework
      • How you can interact with their framework
    • Disads
      • Answer it like others
      • Make offense
      • Even if you loose, case outweighs
  • Strategies
    • Generate as much offense as possible
      • Link turn or impac turn
      • Proactive reason's you're good
    • As much of it written down
      • Just talk
      • File for each type of argument and a list of arguments against it
      • Make everything more efficient
      • Move them to speech docs
    • Uplayer (1 min, hopefully a little bit less)
      • Generally, don't be sketchy
      • Uplayer
      • If winning by a lot or really loosing, uplayer
      • 1AR theory removes the time skew
