08) Tricks

Topic

What are tricks?

Lecture

  • Def of Tricks
    • Small arguments with disproportionately big payoffs
      • Worst thing to do against tricky debater is not being vigiliant
      • If the impact of argument is far greater than length, most likely tricks
    • Tricks debaters are not lazy
      • You need to be on your toes for new tricks
    • Why do people run tricks?
      • They're really strategic
        • Automatic ballot in a few sentences
        • How to identify
        • Have a game plan of what to do in round and afterwords
        • People are bad at responding to tricfks
          • They try to line by line argument
          • Get someone to timesuck into stupid arguments
          • More time to explain why false than explain in the first place
          • Don't realize they're encountering a bad time tradeoff
          • People who are good at philosophy or Ks are suckered into making long response to argument
        • Nuance your arguments for good tradeoffs
  • Different types of tricks
    • Mythical A priori
      • Popular 2008 to 2010
      • People have figures out ways to uplayer
      • A priori
        • Gain offense independent from the standard
        • posessed = evil demon, low investment
      • Skepticism
        • Common in framework arguments
        • Lot of common assumptions that make it easy to run skepticism
        • Ex. All objects are just one object
        • No moral truth behind resolution, so it couldn't be true
    • Spikes
      • Defensive arguments that bundle offenses by AFF
      • Ex. UTIL can't be used
      • Short interp argument that gains a lot of time out of opponent
      • Spikes should be placed on a round to round basis for strategy
    • Burdens
      • You have to jump through hoops to get to burden
      • Burdens are very dense
      • This burden is the most fair interp of the resolution
      • Very difficult to explain why unfair
      • Narrow the debate down to a singular issue you're most prepped on
      • Win offense back to the burden
    • Continguencies
      • If skep is true, all statments are true
    • Theory
      • Paragraph Theory
      • Must run counterplan, must meet xyz conditions
      • Run in undeview of a lot of UTIL AFF
      • interpreted many ways
    • Positively framed resolutions
      • Limit the negative straategy
    • Shells with embedded voters
      • independent voter in theory shells
      • Very easily win the theory debate
  • Running Tricks
    • Making sure that you have really good extensions
      • Explain why the evidence writes the ballot
      • Judges won't like tricks, but will vote on it
    • Stay Fresh
      • Don't recycle arguments
      • Change argument slightly for new fresh implications
    • Don't be obvious
      • Want to catch people not paying attention
    • Tell them they're not allowed ot use examples you provide
      • Trick will narrow down to best, most obvious one
      • Preclude them from arguments that would be responsive to the AFF
    • Prepare for peers
      • Require debaters to debate what they don't have much prep on
  • How to Refute Tricks
    • Refuting A priori
      • Underdevelped, no warrant
      • Impact Turn
        • Turned 3 nibs
    • Skepticism
      • Oppressive
      • Punished really hard for running position
      • Skep can't be resolved, show how your framework resolves
      • More risk
        • philosophic outlayering
      • Fiats
        • Pre fiats come before skep
      • Theory
        • Punish opponent for going for it
    • Spikes
      • Only reply to Spikes if you need to
      • Meta Theory
        • Interp, violation, etc...
        • Must case is shell format
        • Win on time tradeoff
      • Constrained by fairness/education
      • Prep counter interps
    • Burdens
      • If something is wrong with burden, your interp should win the round
        • Have to get good with theory
      • Pick your battles
        • Strategic to engage them on the burden
    • Contingencies
      • XYZ thing is a problem, will try and resolve it
      • Why is there a problem in the first place?
      • There's no relevancy...
      • I solve the problem!
      • The problem doesn't matter as much as this problem
    • Theory
      • Metatheory
      • Ks
        • Deny ability to access theory arguments
      • Or, run both Ks and metatheory.
      • Vote on nibs
      • Turn every argument on opponent
